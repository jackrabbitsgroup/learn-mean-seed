Styling Text
{{pagesFullPath + 'css/css-textstyles'}}
Styling text with CSS is fairly simple and straightforward, for the most part. Once again, W3Schools has a useful reference of Text Styles and Font Styles, and honestly, those pages alone can tell you pretty much everything you need to know.

I won't bother repeating what W3Schools has laid out so nicely, but I will say that you don't need to memorize every single property. Just make sure that you understand color, text-align, font-size, line-height, and font-family. text-decoration, font-style, and font-weight are less important, but still good to know. You probably won't use the others very often; just know that they exist, and you can look them up if you ever need them.

Most of the styles above are fairly self-explanatory, but I would like to point out a particular use for line-height that is often especially helpful: vertical centering.

Horizontally centering text inside a large div is easy with text-align: center;, but vertical centering is notoriously troublesome in comparison. You might think that the vertical-align property would handle it easily, but you'd be wrong. That property almost never works like you want it to where centering is concerned.

The line-height property is perfect for centering if you know the height of your container and if your text only spans a single line. That might sound like a very limited scenario, but it's actually very common - most buttons, for example, satisfy these conditions. So, if those two conditions hold, you can just set line-height equal to the height of your container, and you're done. This is usually much quicker, easier, and less likely to break than any other alternative.
